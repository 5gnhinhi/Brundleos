ABOUT BRUNDLEOS

BrundleOS is the revolutionary operating system created in 1985 by the brilliant scientist Dr. Seth Brundle. Originally designed to interface with his teleportation technology, BrundleOS evolved into something much more powerful - an operating system with advanced genetic analysis capabilities and primitive artificial intelligence.

After Dr. Brundle's tragic disappearance in 1986, the rights to BrundleOS were passed to science journalist Veronica Quaife, who kept the original hard drive containing BrundleOS in storage for over a decade. During a house move in 1998, the hard drive was rediscovered, and through painstaking reverse engineering, Quaife was able to emulate BrundleOS using her custom virtual machine solution: MarquaifVM.
